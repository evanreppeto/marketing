# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Marketing operators, owners, and growth teams at service businesses who need a practical system for generating campaigns, finding leads, reviewing work, and coordinating outbound activity. The current first user is Big Shoulders Restoration, but the product should generalize to many companies through configurable branding, workspace settings, channels, agents, and campaign workflows.

## Product Purpose

Arc is a marketing operations product centered on an agent named Arc. Users can talk to Arc, ask for campaign ideas, generate campaign packages, inspect creative, find leads, understand performance, and approve outbound work before it reaches customers. The product should mature from an internal operating system into a broad SaaS-style product that other companies can adopt without feeling like the interface was custom-made for a single business.

Success looks like a user trusting Arc as a capable teammate while still feeling in control of approvals, brand fit, data, and next actions. The interface should make agentic work legible: what Arc is doing, what needs human review, what is safe to send, and what changed because of the work.

## Brand Personality

Professional, grounded, capable. The product should feel premium and established, with Arc as a focused operator rather than a novelty chatbot. Voice should be direct, calm, and useful: plain-language workflow labels, confident status copy, and no inflated AI spectacle.

## Anti-references

Do not make this look like generic AI-generated SaaS: no neon AI dashboard tropes, purple-blue gradient gloss, bubbly cards, animated spectacle, vague "AI-powered" hero language, decorative glass, or over-styled components that distract from the work. Do not make it feel like a raw admin template, a clone of another productivity app, or a collage of unrelated component libraries. Avoid visual patterns that signal generated UI: repeated identical cards, excessive uppercase micro-labels, faux-premium shadows, noisy pills, and cleverness where a standard control would feel more trustworthy.

## Design Principles

1. Arc is the center, but the operator stays in control.
2. Make agent work inspectable before it is actionable.
3. Prefer established product conventions over decorative novelty.
4. Keep the system customizable without making every workspace feel generic.
5. Use premium restraint: fewer competing treatments, stronger hierarchy, clearer state.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for core text and controls. Support reduced motion, keyboard navigation, visible focus states, and color-independent status communication. Product screens should remain usable for dense daily work: readable dark surfaces, clear touch targets, meaningful labels, and standard interaction patterns for menus, dialogs, tabs, search, and forms.
